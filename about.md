Hosted by the Republic of Moldova

14 - 15 May 2026


About the session

The Session will bring together Ministers for Foreign Affairs and other high-ranking representatives of the member States to provide political guidance and strategic direction to the Organization. As the decision-making body of the Council of Europe, the Committee of Ministers is responsible for setting priorities, adopting decisions and recommendations, supervising the execution of judgments of the European Court of Human Rights, and ensuring the effective implementation of the Organisation’s standards and commitments across its member States.

The meeting will offer an opportunity to assess progress achieved in advancing human rights, democracy and the rule of law, while addressing emerging challenges facing the European continent. 
The 135th Session will also mark the conclusion of the Presidency of the Republic of Moldova of the Committee of Ministers. This moment will provide an occasion to reflect on the priorities advanced during the Presidency and to ensure continuity of action, as the Presidency is formally handed over to Monaco, reaffirming the shared commitment of member States to the Council of Europe’s core values and long-term objectives.

Priorities of the Presidency

The Republic of Moldova joined the Council of Europe on 13 July 1995. Taking over for a second time the presidency of the Committee of Ministers of the Organisation in the year of its 30th membership anniversary is both highly symbolic and of utmost importance.

In 30 years of Council of Europe membership, our democracy has been consolidated, the rule of law reformed, the protection of human rights improved and extended, and the civic space strengthened. Together, we have created new institutions, put in place new policies, raised awareness, inspired transformations and stirred new aspirations. The Council of Europe has been a genuine and trusted partner for the Republic of Moldova, which stood by us when challenges had to be tackled and innovative solutions had to be found.

The Republic of Moldova joined the Council of Europe at a time when European values were thriving, perspectives were bright, and the future of Europe was unclouded. We will be exercising our presidency at a time in which war has returned to Europe due to the Russian Federation’s unjust and unprovoked aggression against Ukraine, a New Democratic Pact is needed to counter democratic backsliding, the rule of law is being increasingly challenged, and the very essence of fundamental human rights and freedoms are misused as part of foreign information manipulation and interference. “A perfect storm” was how Secretary General Alain Berset described the new reality in which we are evolving in his Report for 2025.

The new realities in which we are operating make our Organisation even more significant, highlight the need for renewed responsibility and commitment for its core values, and call for resolute actions to strengthen our collective response to the challenges to its fundamental principles.

In exercising its mandate, as presidency of the Committee of Ministers, the Republic of Moldova will endeavour – under the motto For a Europe of Peace and Resilience – to facilitate the convergence of the Council of Europe activities towards reaching its objectives and enhancing its capacities and acquiring new tools to better adapt to shifting realities while preserving the key importance of its essential values.

The presidency will be guided by three major axes:

Support for Ukraine and the accountability for the crime of aggression committed by the Russian Federation – The Presidency will facilitate the Council of Europe’s support to Ukraine through capacity-building, monitoring and co-operation initiatives and the strengthening of its mechanisms to ensure accountability, including the operationalisation of the Special Tribunal and setting up the Claims Commission as a part of the compensation mechanism, the first step of which is the Register.
Support for the work on the New Democratic Pact for Europe. The Moldovan Presidency will support the Council of Europe’s process towards a New Democratic Pact for Europe. From our experience as a still young democracy, we have learned that democracy is rather a process and not a destination, that preserving and defending it requires a genuine commitment and consistent actions, and that ensuring trust or rebuilding it where necessary is a key element in the process. At a time when the whole of Europe is facing unprecedented challenges, when new tactics are being employed to undermine democratic processes, old tools/conventional instruments/existing mechanisms are no longer enough to protect our democratic values. We must adapt, innovate and find effective solutions to defend freedom, uphold the rule of law and protect fundamental rights.
Strengthening our response to disinformation, including foreign information manipulation and interference.
